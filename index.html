Are cryptocurrencies investment similar to gold?

By Vineet Sachdev

In the last few years there have many media reports which said that investments in cryptocurrencies are similar to digital gold. The meteoric rise in the value of cryptocurrencies in the last decade have attracted hordes of new investors towards them. Since the production of cryptocurrency is limited in number, they have been compared to gold for storing value for the investors. We discuss below whether that comparison is valid.

Not good investment during downtimes

We look at the historical price movement of gold and compare it to the price movement of well-known cryptocurrency - Bitcoin and the S& P 500 equity index to understand how these asset classes are related. A 30-day rolling average of the data is used to make the comparison.

The chart shows that while the price of Bitcoin is very volatile, its movement has mostly been in correlation to the S & P equity index i.e., showing increase in price when the equity markets are rising and falling when they are down. In contrast the price movement of gold have shown negative correlation. While price of Bitcoin and S& P index have fallen by around 50% and 20% respectively till date since the beginning of the year as major central banks increase interest rates and war in Ukraine rages on, gold has gained around 1%. This indicates that cryptocurrencies are not good hedging instrument as gold is for investors to preserve their investments during times of uncertainty.

Preservation of initial investment amount

Year end data on prices for bitcoin and gold for past 5 years shows that while gains from cryptocurrency investment have been higher on yearly basis in many years, investment in this asset class also has the potential to destroy initial investments too. In comparison- while gold prices may not have grown much, it has never resulted in much destruction of investor investment. This places gold as a much safer bet for investments over longer term than cryptocurrencies.

The above analysis shows that comparing cryptocurrencies to digital gold may not be right since they does not share some of the basic properties of gold investment for their investors.
